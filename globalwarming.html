Global warming is driving a range of environmental challenges that impact both natural ecosystems and human societies. Rising temperatures are melting polar ice caps and glaciers, leading to sea level rise that threatens coastal areas worldwide. This increase in global temperatures also intensifies weather patterns, resulting in more frequent and severe storms, droughts, and heatwaves that disrupt agriculture, water supplies, and local economies.

In addition, global warming is worsening air quality and contributing to a higher risk of wildfires in vulnerable regions. Changes in climate patterns can destabilize ecosystems, reduce biodiversity, and negatively affect food security due to diminished crop yields. Addressing these issues calls for comprehensive global efforts to cut greenhouse gas emissions and shift toward sustainable energy and environmental practices.
